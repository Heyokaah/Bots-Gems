10 Parts of Speech Notes

Secondary 4 Reference Syllabus

Part 1 Noun (N.)

Noun

The name of a person, place, thing, animal, or abstract concept.

Countable: chairs, children, books, facilities

Uncountable: advice, information, equipment, knowledge, beauty

E.g., "John lives in Sai Kung."

Part 2 Verb (V.)

Verb

A word that shows action, state of being, or physical occurrence.

Action Verb: run, started, complete

State of Being: is, was, seem, feel

E.g., "The baby is smiling."

Part 3 Adjective (Adj.)

Adjective

Describes, qualifies, or modifies a noun or pronoun.

Usage: Junk food is cheap but unhealthy.

-ed vs -ing forms: He felt disappointed versus The movie was boring.

E.g., "The fat boy walked."

Part 4 Adverb (Adv.)

Adverb

Modifies or describes a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.

Modifying verbs: A snail moves slowly.

Modifying adjectives: She is very lazy.

E.g., "The protective screen is put temporarily."

Part 5 Pronoun (Pron.)

Pronoun

Used in place of a noun to avoid unnecessary repetition.

Personal: he, she, it, they, us, him

Reflexive / Intensive: myself, himself, itself

E.g., "The manager himself signed the contract."

Part 6 Preposition (Prep.)

Preposition

Shows temporal, spatial, or logical relationships between nouns/pronouns and other words.

Examples: round, inside, before, in, with, on, under

E.g., "Some employers fail to provide screens round machines."

Part 7 Conjunction (Conj.)

Conjunction

Connects clauses, words, sentences, or coordinating phrases.

Coordinating: but, and, or, yet, so

Subordinating: because, although, since, unless

E.g., "John lives in Sai Kung but he works in Central."

Part 8 Article (Art.)

Article

Determines whether a noun is specific or general.

Indefinite: a, an (non-specific, general)

Definite: the (specifically pointed to or previously mentioned)

E.g., "She bought an interesting book."

Part 9 Demonstrative

Demonstrative

Identifies or points out a specific noun or pronoun.

Singular: this, that

Plural: these, those

E.g., "Those books belong to the library."

Part 10 Interjection

Interjection

An abrupt remark, exclamation, or vocalized emotion.

Examples: Wow! Ouch! Oh! Gosh!

E.g., "Ouch! The soup is burning hot!"
