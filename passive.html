Active vs. Passive Formulas

Passive voice transitions require moving the direct object of the active sentence to the subject position, and then inserting the auxiliary verb to be matched to the original tense and matching subject plurality, followed by the past participle of the verb.

Simple Present
Active: Subject + Verb(s/es) + Object
Am / Is / Are + Past Participle
Present Continuous
Active: Subject + Am/Is/Are + V-ing + Object
Am / Is / Are + being + Past Participle
Present Perfect
Active: Subject + Has/Have + Past Participle + Object
Has / Have + been + Past Participle
Simple Past
Active: Subject + Past Tense Verb + Object
Was / Were + Past Participle
Past Continuous
Active: Subject + Was/Were + V-ing + Object
Was / Were + being + Past Participle
Past Perfect
Active: Subject + Had + Past Participle + Object
Had + been + Past Participle
Simple Future
Active: Subject + Will + Base Verb + Object
Will + be + Past Participle
